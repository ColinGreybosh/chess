Openings

Moves

Ruy Lopez (Spanish)

One of the oldest chess openings in history, dating back to before the 15th century - playing this opening as white allows the player to develop their bishop and attack black's knight.

Sicilian Defense

Once white plays his King's pawn, black responds with his C file pawn instead of his Queen's pawn. This position creates dissymmetry and opens the board to improvisation and odd development. The open-endedness of the Sicilian makes it popular between masters.

King's Gambit

White playing the King's pawn allows him/her to quickly begin utilizing both the bishop and knight at the cost of a pawn. This opening originated in the 19th century, but is not too popular anymore due to black's ability to decide to ignore the center pawns.

Queen's Gambit

The Queen's Gambit opening allows white to open up the board and develop their pieces quickly in exchange for a pawn. Black can either ignore the exposed pawn in the center, or capture it.

King's Indian Defense

This risky opening, playing as black, allows white to take the center of the board. By giving up this position early in the game, you are given the chance later on to receive an advantage from a misplay.
